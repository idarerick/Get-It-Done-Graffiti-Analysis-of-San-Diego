Analysis

The graffiti data used for this project came from data.sandiego.gov; the range of data begins at 5-20-16 and covers all reports until 10-20-18. We saw an opportunity to explore the relationship between graffiti reports across the 9 San Diego districts, the use of the Get It Done app, and a local program called Writerz Bloq. Writerz Bloq is a program that allows street artists to create and display their work legally. The leaflet has a heat map layer that can be toggled, and markers for the obundary lines of each of the 9 districts in San Diego that can aslo be toggled. Writerz Bloq is marked on the map and we can see that it is at the epicenter of one of the reported graffiti hotspots. The bar map shows the average number of days for each district between when an incident of graffiti is reported, and when the city completes the cleanup. A key takeaway is ("that district ?8 has both the highest average and one of the highest concentrations of reports"). The three pie charts break down the completion time of each request per district; broken into increments of 0-5 days, 6-18 days, and 18 days and up. There have been 321 reported incidences that where completed the same day they were reported, and the maximum number of days taken to complete a request is 646 in district 8. This chart shows the disperagy of reports made between districts ?8 and ?4, which could acount for the higher average completion rate of district ?8.
